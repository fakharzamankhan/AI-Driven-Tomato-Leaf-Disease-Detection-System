Tomato Care Tips

Strong daily care habits are the easiest way to prevent from disease and improve fruits quality. Use these practical tips to keep your Tomato plants healthy through the season.

Watering
  • Water at the base of each plant to keep foliage dry and lower fungal risk.
  • Water in the morning so any moisture dries before evening.
  • Avoid overhead watering whenever possible; drip irrigation or a soaker hose works best.
  • Keep soil evenly moist, not waterlogged. In warm weather, aim for about 1-2 inches of water per week.
Sunlight & Air
  • Tomatoes perform best with 6-8 hours of direct sunlight each day.
  • Space plants about 24-36 inches apart to improve airflow and reduce humidity around leaves.
  • Prune lower leaves and selected suckers to increase ventilation and reduce contact with soil-borne pathogens.
Disease Prevention
  • Early Blight / Late Blight: Remove infected leaves promptly, avoid wet foliage, and use a labeled copper-based fungicide when needed.
  • Leaf Mold: Reduce humidity, water at the base, and maintain good ventilation. In severe cases, apply an approved fungicide such as chlorothalonil.
  • Rotate crops and avoid planting tomatoes in the same bed year after year.
  • Add mulch around plants to reduce soil splash, stabilize moisture, and keep roots cooler.
Provide Healthy Soil
  • Work compost into the soil before planting tomatoes.
  • Side-dress plantings with compost midseason to add nutrients.
  • Apply a fertilizer specifically designed for tomatoes every two weeks during the growing season, starting with planting time.
